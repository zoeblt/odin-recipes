You can go a few differnt ways with a stuffed pepper rescipe. Italian; ground beef with italian spices, perhaps cooked in red sause, topped with mozzerella. Mexican; ground beef cooked with taco seasoning, stuffed with cream cheese and topped with sharp chedder and jalapeños. Greek; Ground beef or chicken seasoned with garam masala, topped with feta, tomato and cucumber salad and a bit of tasiki. Today we will be trying our hand at my personal favorite, Mexican stuffed peppers.

But before we start let me give some commentary on the different colors of bell peppers. My mom always tells me I'm crazy when I say that yellow bell peppers are my favorite. Most people think there is no difference between the colors. Flavor wise, thats mostly true but yellow is my favorite because of the texture. Green peppers are the most crunchy, to me a little too crunchy. They need to be baked longer to get to a better texture. Yellow ones are nice and crispy and sometimes even slightly sweeter. Orange is pretty much the same as yellow. Then theres red. Raw red peppers are amazing. Cooked red peppers are a different story for me. To me they are akin to tomatos. I love raw tomatos, the texture when cooked? No thank you. If red is cooked for too long, the skin starts to fall off and it starts to get a little mushy. If you don't find that texture, thats the pepper for you. Though if you're like me, stick with yellow and orange. Alright, now onto the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 6 bell peppers
  • 2lbs of ground beef
  • 2 blocks of cream cheese
  • taco seasoning of your choice
  • shredded chedder cheese
  • 1 Diced jalapeño (optional)

Steps

  1. Pre-head ovan to 350
  2. Cut bell peppers in half and de-vain and de-seed. Set out on a baking sheet.
  3. Heat up your skillet and cook ground beef until theres just a little pink left. Drain the fat and return to heat. Add in taco seasoning and cook until there is no more pink. Cover and set aside.
  4. Fill halfed peppers with a layer of cream cheese (however thick or thin you prefer)
  5. Add your ground beef on top of the cream cheese.
  6. Top with your shredded chedder and jalapeños
  7. Finally, place in ovan and cook for 15-20mins, until cheese on top is melted
  8. Serve as is or top with your favorite salsa!
